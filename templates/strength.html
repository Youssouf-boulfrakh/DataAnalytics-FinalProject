Age/Curing

Concrete curing is the process of maintaining adequate moisture and proper temperature over time. The cement within concrete must remain hydrated in order to properly settle and harden over time.

Compressive Strength

The quality of concrete is often assessed through compressive strength testing. Concrete is sampled from a fresh mixture and made into a batch of test cylinders. The test cylinders are cured for a length of time at different intervals, often 7, 14, 21, and 28 days. When a test cylinder has aged it is then compressed in a machine and compressive strength is recorded once failure of the cylinder is met.
